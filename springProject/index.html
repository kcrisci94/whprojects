Our Solar System

The sun is the center of our universe. Specifically, it is a star, or ball of extremely hot gasses held together by its own gravity. The sun is about 10,000 degrees Fahrenheit and can hold approximately 1,300,000 Earths inside its mass. All that mass also means a strong gravitational pull, which forces the Earth and all the other planets in the solar system to orbit around it, making it possible for life to exist. For more info, visit https://www.nasa.gov/sun.
